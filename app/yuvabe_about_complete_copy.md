## **Values — Not Slogans. Standards.**

### **01 — Care**

Be thoughtful with people, product decisions, and the work itself. This means saying the uncomfortable thing early — not after the roadmap is already full.

**In practice:** On a project mid-build, research showed the core user assumption was wrong. The sprint was paused and the finding presented — two weeks before a key milestone. The client was frustrated. Eighteen months later, the product was still alive; the original direction would have killed it.

*Note: PLACEHOLDER — Replace this story with a real project moment from the team. Keep it under 3 sentences: what did the team do, why was it uncomfortable, what was the result.*

 

### **02 — Courage**

Say the hard thing early, make the sharper call, and avoid drift. If the strategy is wrong, it's better to say so at week two than week twelve.

**In practice:** A client came with a fully scoped brief. Three conversations in, the scoping was found to be wrong — they were solving a distribution problem with a product solution. The team walked them back to first principles before writing a single line of code. They shipped six months later with a product that matched the market.

*Note: PLACEHOLDER — Replace with a real story where the team pushed back on a brief or changed project direction.*

 

### **03 — Creativity**

Find simpler, better ways through hard product and technical problems — not around them. The best solution is usually the one that felt impossible until it was obvious.

**In practice:** KittyKat needed product-to-model fashion imagery at scale. The obvious answer was to optimise the photoshoot process. The creative answer was to eliminate it — a generative AI pipeline that turned product shots into styled model imagery without a studio. The constraint became the solution.

## **The Diagnosis — Why Building is Broken**

Building has never been cheaper. AI has made the mechanics of shipping faster than ever before. What has not gotten easier: knowing what is worth building. Validating the right assumption. Staying connected to the original thinking once a team starts moving fast.

*"Most agencies are built to execute. They need a brief to function. The thinking — and the risk — stays with the founder."*

Yuvabe is built differently. Strategy, design, engineering, and growth work happens in one connected loop — with the same people, from the first conversation to the first traction signal.

They are doing the same work faster. | What Yuvabe means   AI has changed what is possible in strategy, design, engineering, and growth — and we have rebuilt how we work around that. Faster research cycles. Leaner prototyping. Systems that learn from user behaviour from day one. Not a feature bolted on at the end — a foundation. |
