## Website Strategy

- The site must clearly communicate the shift into an AI-first studio serving startups.
- Position Yuvabe as the partner that helps founders decide what to build, execute fast, and iterate toward traction through product, engineering, and growth marketing.
- Favor clarity, momentum, and trust over feature dumping or agency-style fluff.

## Default Page Structure

1. Navigation with a clear primary CTA
2. Hero — short headline, one supporting paragraph, one primary conversion action
3. Proof or credibility section to reduce founder risk
4. Capabilities/services framed around outcomes, not internal departments
5. Process or operating-model showing how Yuvabe helps founders decide, build, and learn
6. Work, case studies, or evidence section
7. FAQ or objection-handling block when needed
8. Final CTA section with one strong next step

## Content Rules

- Keep the primary story above the fold on desktop.
- Every section must answer a founder question: why trust you, what do you do, how do you work, why now, what happens next.
- Use AI-first language as practical leverage: faster iteration, sharper prioritization, smarter automation, stronger economics.
- Keep growth marketing explicit — the site should not read like a product-only studio.
- Each page must have one dominant CTA path and minimal decision noise.

## About Page Direction

- Answer four things clearly: who Yuvabe is, how Yuvabe works, why founders trust the team, who Yuvabe is best suited to help.
- More personal and conversational than the homepage, while staying concise and founder-relevant.
- Use proof and operating style to build trust; avoid turning it into either a manifesto or a services page.
- Route About-page wording and tone through `/copywriting`.

## Surface Guidance

- Use premium surfaces for conversion-critical layers: mobile navigation, hero proof, featured case studies, final CTA framing.
- Keep premium surface usage selective; too many frosted or elevated panels reduce hierarchy.
